Documentation for Researchers

This section outlines how to work with the RCTrials project for researchers looking to gather survey responses from trial participants.

Registering an account

First, you must create an account on the site. To do that, navigate to the Registration page and fill out the form.

Configuring a trial

Once you are logged in, you can specify the parameters of your trial by clicking the "New Trial" button from the Dashboard screen.

To define the parameters of your trial there are three main steps - "Basic Info", "Groups", and "Surveys".

The "Basic Info" step asks you to provide the "Trial Title", which is used on the Dashboard screen to help you identify your trials. The "Registration" dates, which are used to determine when participants are allowed to register into the trial. If the closing date is left blank, then participants may continue to register indefinitely. Finally, the "Trial" dates, which are used to determine when then actual trial is taking place. This is useful when there are key dates or milestones during the trial. Leaving the ending date blank will allow participants to remain in the trial indefinitely and continue submitting questionnaire responses.

The "Groups" step allows you to configure how to split up the participants into groups. When new users register into your trial they will be randomly assigned into one of the created groups. On the next step you can define which surveys will be administered to which group.

The "Surveys" step allows you to define the surveys for the trial, and which groups will receive which surveys at what time.

First, provide the "Survey Name" that will be shown to the participant when they receive the survey.

Then, specify when the survey should be delivered to the participant. "Pretest" is delevered at the trial start date, or immediately upon registration if the trial is in progress. "During Treatment" will repeatedly deliver the survey allowing the participant to submit responses every n days. "Posttest" is delivered at the trial end date.

You can then specify which groups should receive the given survey. This can be useful when you do not wish to deliver certain surveys to a control or experiment group.

Then you need to define the questions for this survey. Each question has the "Question Text", and depending on the "Question Type" there may be a need for "Question Options". Options are provided in a pipe ("|") delimited format, and the input control will adjust to the available options. For example a radio button input that allows the participant to select one of many options will display as many radio buttons as there are pipe delimited options.

You can create as many surveys for the trial as necessary by clicking the "add new survey" button.

When you are finished, review that everything is correct and click "Finish". If the entry was successful you should receive a 4-digit alphanumeric ID. This is the Trial ID that your participants will use to register into your trial.

Collecting data

To collect data from your trial participants using the hosted solution - direct the trial participants to download the RCTrials App and register into your trial using the given Trial ID to submit survey responses. More detailed instructions can be found in the documentation for participants.

If you wish to collect data from your trial participants using your own solution integrated with the RCTrials API - direct your developers to the documentation on integrating with the API.
